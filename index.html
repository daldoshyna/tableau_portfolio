Introduction

In this project, I analysed music streaming data to explore how tracks, artists, and genres relate to popularity and listener behaviour over time. My goal was to examine these relationships from multiple analytical perspectives and communicate the findings through clear, engaging visualisations.

The analysis is based on data from Spotify, one of the worldâ€™s most widely used music streaming platforms. The dataset includes information about tracks, artists, popularity metrics, release dates, genres, and explicit content, which makes it well suited for studying long-term trends in music and listener preferences. The source of the dataset is available here.

In my portfolio, I approached the data from three main perspectives and created six distinct visualisations:

- Tracks
Viz 1: Number of Tracks released per Year: Overall and Explicit
Viz 2: Track and Artist Popularity for Explicit and Non-explicit Tracks
Viz 3: Track Popularity vs. Track Duration

- Artists
Viz 4: Top 10 Artists with the highest Popularity Rating per Genre
Viz 5: Artist's Popularity vs. Number of Followers

- Music Genges
Viz 6: Top Genre explorer

All visualisations were created using Tableau. To maintain visual consistency and align the design with the subject matter, I used the Spotify colour palette throughout the project. The source of the palette is available here.

1. Number of Tracks released per Year: Overall and Explicit

Context & Question

At the beginning, I wanted to summarise the time-sensitive trends of the changes in the number of overall and explicit tracks released each year. Is their number increasing? If so, how rapidly?

Design Decisions

Chart type:
- Chart 1: Dual-axis Bar and Line chart. These viz types are most suitable for the timeseries, and their simultaneous use helps to showcase two distinct instances at the same time.
- Chart 2: Pie chart. This viz type works well for displaying fractions of a whole, which is exactly what I intended to do here.

Interactive features:
- By chosing a particular year on the bar chart you can directly view the fraction of the explicit songs in that year on the pie chart.
- Interactive tooltip.

Key Insights

I discovered that the number of both overall and explicit tracks tends to increase throughout the years, even though there was a noticeable decrease around 2021-2022.
The fraction (% of the overall songs) of the explicit songs also gradually increases.

Data Source: Spotify Global Music Dataset

2. Track and Artist Popularity for Explicit and Non-explicit Tracks

Context & Question

Here I wanted to compare the popularity ratings for explicit vs. non-explicit songs throughout the years. Which tend to be more popular?

Design Decisions

Chart type:
Line chart. This viz type is suitable for the timeseries and also allows to make comparisons of multiple instances (lines). I also used a window function here to smooth the lines a little bit so that they would showcase the trends more plainly.

Visual encodings: position, color, and text labels.

Interactive features:
- Metric selector allows to choose between two metrics of popularity: track popularity rating and artist popularity rating.
- Interactive tooltip.

Key Insights

I discovered that both track and artist populatity ratings tend to be higher for explicit songs. It's also notable that the artist popularity rating tends to be generally higher than track popularity rating.

Data Source: Spotify Global Music Dataset

3. Track Popularity vs. Track Duration

Context & Question

Is there a correlation or some kind of other non-linear relationship between the popularity of a track and its duration?

Design Decisions

Chart type:
- Scatter Plot. This viz type is the most suitable to showcase the relationship between some two variables.
- Trend line, which helps to summarize the relationship between the variables and draws attantion to the trends. A simple linear trend seems to fit this data the best.

Visual encodings: position, color.

Interactive features:
- Genre selector allows to filter the genres of the tracks.
- Time period selector allows to select a five-year period.
- Interactive tooltip that includes information about each point's track name and artist's name.

Key Insights

I discovered that there seems to be a weak linear correlation between the two variables. It is stronger for some genre and time-period pairs, and weaker for others. For some categories, the correlation is positive (Electronic, 2021-2025, for example), and for some it is negative (Metal, 2016-2020). It would be interesting to further analyze the relationship between the two variables with more precise analytical tests.

Data Source: Spotify Global Music Dataset

4. Top 10 Artists with the highest Popularity Rating per Genre

Context & Question

Next, I decided to move on from the topic of tracks and explore the information about the artists, specifically the most popular ones. What are top 10 most popular artists in each genre and overall? What are their popularity rating and number of followers?

Design Decisions

Chart type:
Dual-axis horizontal bar chart. This viz type helps to simultaneously view numeral instances (artists) and compare them by two distinct indicators (popularity rating and number of followers).

Visual encodings: position, color, and text labels.

Interactive features:
- Genre selector allows to filter the genres of the artists.
- Interactive tooltip.

Key Insights

I discovered the most popular artists in different genres. It was interesting to see and compare the familiar artists by their popularity rating and number of followers. It's also notable here that the higher popularity rating does not always mean higher number of followers.

Data Source: Spotify Global Music Dataset

5. Artist's Popularity vs. Number of Followers

Context & Question

A natural question arises from the previous visualization: is there a noticeable correlation or some kind of other non-linear relationship between artist's popularity and number of followers?

Design Decisions

Chart type:
- Scatter Plot. This viz type is the most suitable to showcase the relationship between the two variables.
- Trend line, which helps to summarize the relationship between the variables and draws attantion to the trends. Here an exponential trend seems to fit data the best.

Visual encodings: position, color.

Interactive features:
- Genre selector allows to filter the genres of the tracks.
- Interactive tooltip that includes information about artist's name for each point.

Key Insights

I discovered that the relationship between an artist's popularity and their number of followers seems to follow an exponential distribution. It seems to be true for most (if not all) genres. But, of course, much more extensive research is needed to actually verify this observation:)

Data Source: Spotify Global Music Dataset

6. Top Genre explorer

Context & Question

The last topic I wanted to explore was musical genres, what are the top ones with the most tracks? What number of tracks was released in those genres throughout the years? What popularity rating do these genres have?

Design Decisions

Chart type:
- Chart 1: Treemap, which showcases all of the music genres and their number of tracks.
- Chart 2: Line chart. It works very well for timeseries and draws attention to general time-sensitive trends in the changes in the number of track released per top genre.
- Chart 3: Bar chart, used for comparison of the popularity ratings of top genres.

Interactive features:
- Selector allows to view the results for either top 3 genres or top 5 ones.
- Interactive tooltip.

Key Insights

The top genres with the highest number of songs released are: Pop, Hip Hop / Rap, Soundrack, Rock, Country / Folk.
The number of Pop songs very noticeably increased in the period of 2005-2015. The biggest number of Hip Hop / Rap tracks were released in 2018-2020. Interestingly, the most Rock songs were released in the 1990s, and in that period Rock was definitely a top 1 genre.
The most popular genres (based on their popularity rating) are Hip Hop / Rap, Pop, and Rock. Even though Soundtrack is in top 3 in terms of number of tracks, it is also the least popular genre.

Data Source: Spotify Global Music Dataset
